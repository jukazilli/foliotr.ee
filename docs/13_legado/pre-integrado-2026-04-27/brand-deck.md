## 1. Brand in One Sentence

FolioTree turns professional trajectory into clear, lively, fast-to-understand evidence.

## 2. Mission

Help people organize, update, and show their professional value in a way that is clear, visual, and memorable.

## 3. Vision

Become the most contemporary, vibrant, and useful way to turn professional trajectory into real digital presence.

## 4. Purpose

Make professional value visible in a few seconds.

## 5. Problem Framing

The main problem is not simply the lack of a resume or the lack of a portfolio.

The deeper problem is this:
people constantly rewrite and reorganize their professional story across different channels, in inconsistent ways, with weak proof and poor speed of understanding.

This creates friction for:

- recruiters
- clients
- collaborators
- the professionals themselves

## 6. Product Interpretation

The current product interpretation should be:

- one central professional identity base
- structured professional data, proof, and narrative
- multiple output formats from one source

Possible outputs include:

- public pages
- resumes
- portfolios
- versions for specific opportunities

This interpretation is important.
Do not flatten the product into only one of these outputs.

## 7. Strategic Position

### Positioning statement

FolioTree is the platform that turns a scattered professional trajectory into a clear, lively, and convincing presence for recruiters, clients, and opportunities.

### Short strategic contrast

LinkedIn shows. FolioTree proves.

### Strategic interpretation

LinkedIn can support visibility and networking.
FolioTree should support evidence, clarity, speed, and value perception.

## 8. Big Brand Idea

Your trajectory has value. FolioTree makes that value appear.

Alternative phrasing:
FolioTree is where trajectory becomes perceived value.

## 9. Brand Essence

**Living professional evidence.**

## 10. Audience

### Primary audience

Digital and knowledge workers who need to present themselves better, including:

- developers
- designers
- product managers
- analysts
- freelancers
- consultants
- creators
- professionals in career transition

### Audience needs

This audience wants to:

- look more professional
- be understood faster
- present proof, not only titles
- adapt their narrative to different contexts
- stop rewriting everything from scratch

## 11. What the Brand Must Feel Like

FolioTree should feel:

- vibrant
- clear
- lively
- useful
- structured
- visually expressive
- professional without being stiff
- current without being trend-fragile

## 12. What the Brand Must Not Feel Like

FolioTree must not feel:

- bureaucratic
- cold
- generic
- overly corporate
- visually chaotic
- childish
- meme-driven
- like a generic builder
- like a standard hiring platform

## 13. Archetype

### Primary archetype

**Friend**

Why:

- approachable
- communicative
- easy to understand
- welcoming

### Secondary archetype

**Creator**

Why:

- transforms information into presence
- values presentation and identity
- turns raw trajectory into vivid evidence

### Combined interpretation

Friend + Creator = a brand that simplifies, energizes, and transforms.

## 14. Personality

If FolioTree were a person, it would be:

- cheerful
- energetic
- communicative
- intelligent
- easy to understand
- visually striking
- professional without stiffness
- able to speak to Gen Z, Gen Y, and Gen X without sounding forced

### Personality shorthand

**vibrant, clear, bold, close, intelligent, current**

## 15. Values

### 1. Clarity over noise

Professional value should be easy to understand.

### 2. Expression with structure

More personality, but with order and legibility.

### 3. Continuous evolution

A career changes, so the way it is presented should evolve too.

### 4. Fast value perception

The brand should always favor fast understanding and immediate impact.

### 5. User control

The user should control narrative, versions, and public presentation.

## 19. Short Manifesto

You are not just another profile.

Your trajectory has context.
Your achievements carry weight.
Your projects, results, and experiences say more than a list of roles.

Even so, most professional lives are still scattered, fragmented, and difficult to present the right way.

FolioTree exists to change that.

Here, trajectory gains structure, presence, and impact.
Everything that proves your value lives in one place, ready to become a page, resume, portfolio, or version for the right opportunity.

The world already has enough profiles.
What it lacks is clear evidence.

FolioTree exists to make your value appear in a few seconds.

## 22. Product Language Guidance

Preferred user-facing nouns:

- Profile = central source
- Versions = context-specific adaptations
- Page = public page output
- Resume = resume output
- Highlights = strongest achievements
- Proof = links, cases, results, metrics
- Snapshot = fixed version for a specific opportunity

Avoid:

- technical builder terms
- overly abstract naming
- internal architecture terminology in user-facing copy

## 23. Design Direction

### Core design formula

**Linktree in energy. FolioTree in depth.**

### Visual mood

**bold + clean + modular + vibrant**

### The interface should communicate

- agility
- evidence
- updated identity
- personal expression
- accessible professionalism
- fast understanding

### Composition guidance

- strong grid
- large cards
- clear sections
- generous spacing
- high scanability
- visible proof modules for projects, achievements, links, results, and metrics

### Typography guidance

- sans serif with personality
- large headlines
- short subheads
- clear contrast between title, support text, and CTA
- high readability

### Color guidance

Approved direction:

- tonal families from `docs/tonal system.md`
- light `paper` base with expressive `orange`, `pink`, `cream`, `green`, `cyan`, and `peach`
- hard black borders and shadows as a recognizable LINKFOLIO visual device
- Lime 500 as signature energetic CTA/headline accent
- Green, Cyan, Violet, Coral, and Brown as semantic support colors
- high contrast with same-family dark typography when used over expressive sections

Not yet approved:

- final brand identity system beyond the current public UI cut
- final logo system beyond the current geometric mark/wordmark

### Motion guidance

- quick
- responsive
- snappy
- used to reinforce progress, flow, and clarity

### Graphic elements

- badges
- chips
- highlight blocks
- modular shapes
- simple forms
- strong corners
- occasional gradients, used with restraint

## 24. Creative Guardrails

Always preserve:

- clarity
- speed of understanding
- proof
- structure
- expressive professionalism

Never drift into:

- cold enterprise HR aesthetics
- chaotic creator-platform aesthetics
- vague copy
- generic template language
- static resume-only framing
- product feature invention

### Fold 3

- experiences

- links

- versions

- examples
- before/after
- sample profiles

## 26. Strategic Differentiation

FolioTree should not be interpreted as a better-looking LinkedIn profile.
It should not be interpreted as a generic page builder for careers.

Its value comes from combining:

- centralization
- structure
- versioning
- proof
- fast perception of value
- expressive public presentation

- use this file to expand strategy, not to replace the core
- avoid making roadmap claims not documented here
